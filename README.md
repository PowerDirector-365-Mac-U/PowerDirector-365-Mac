## 🔍 Overview

<a href="#powerDirector365">PowerDirector 365</a> serves content creators, semi-professional videographers, small production teams, and anyone who needs more capability than iMovie provides but who isn't working in a professional Premiere Pro or Final Cut Pro environment.

The <a href="#powerDirector365">YouTube and social content creator use case</a> is PowerDirector's primary market — creators who produce videos regularly and need AI tools that save time on common post-production tasks, a large effects library for engaging visual content, and fast export for multiple platform formats. The subscription content model provides continuously fresh templates and effects. The <a href="#powerDirector365">event videographer workflow</a> uses multicam editing for wedding and event coverage, stabilization for handheld footage, and color grading for consistent visual style — professional production features at consumer software pricing.

The <a href="#powerDirector365">corporate and business video production</a> use case creates training videos, product demonstrations, and marketing content — with motion graphics templates, animated lower thirds, and the complete production toolset for polished corporate video without the complexity of professional broadcast tools. <a href="#powerDirector365">365 subscription content</a> provides access to a library of stock footage, music, sound effects, templates, and effects that updates continuously — supplementing original production content with professional-grade stock resources.

<a href="#powerDirector365">360 degree video editing</a> handles VR and 360 footage from action cameras and dedicated 360 cameras — enabling spherical video editing, title placement in 360 space, and export to 360 video platforms. <a href="#powerDirector365">Screen recording integration</a> captures Mac screen content directly for tutorial production, software demonstrations, and any content that combines screen capture with camera footage.

<a href="#powerDirector365">Fast export with hardware acceleration</a> uses the Mac's GPU for H.264, HEVC, and other codec export — significantly reducing export time compared to software encoding, making high-resolution export practical within a production timeline. <a href="#powerDirector365">Cross-platform consistency</a> — PowerDirector 365 shares the same subscription and project format across Mac and Windows, enabling teams that use mixed operating systems to share project files.

<details>
<summary>Does PowerDirector 365 work on Apple Silicon?</summary>

</details>

<details>
<summary>Can I record my screen in PowerDirector 365?</summary>

Yes. Screen recording is integrated for capturing Mac screen content for tutorial and software demonstration production.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Does PowerDirector 365 share project files between Mac and Windows?</summary>

Yes. The cross-platform subscription and project format enables sharing between Mac and Windows installations.

</details>

<details>
<summary>How does PowerDirector 365 compare to Final Cut Pro?</summary>

PowerDirector 365 includes more AI-powered effects and subscription stock content. Final Cut Pro offers deeper integration with macOS and Apple hardware and a one-time purchase model. Both are capable video editors; the choice depends on workflow priorities.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Does PowerDirector 365 have motion tracking?</summary>

Yes. AI Motion Tracking follows moving subjects and attaches graphics, text, or effects that follow automatically.

</details>

<details>
<summary>What is the audio ducking feature?</summary>

Audio ducking automatically reduces background music volume when speech is detected, maintaining dialogue intelligibility over music without manual keyframing.

</details>
